## How to Use This Handbook

### For Interview Preparation

1. **Assess Your Level**: Review the checklists to identify your current level
2. **Study by Topic**: Go through questions relevant to the role you're applying for
3. **Practice Scenarios**: Work through hands-on scenarios to build troubleshooting skills
4. **Design Exercises**: Practice architecture design problems (draw diagrams!)
5. **Review Roadmap**: Follow the study roadmap for structured preparation

### For Interviewers

- Use questions as a starting point for technical discussions
- Adapt scenarios to your team's tech stack
- Reference architecture exercises for system design rounds

### Study Strategy

- **30 Days**: Focus on fundamentals, one topic per week
- **60 Days**: Deep dive into your weak areas, practice scenarios
- **90 Days**: Full coverage, mock interviews, architecture practice

## Content Structure

### Questions Format

Each question includes:
- **Question**: Clear, interview-style question
- **Difficulty**: Junior / Mid / Senior level indicator
- **Answer**: Detailed explanation with context
- **Follow-ups**: Related questions to explore deeper
- **Real-world Context**: When you might encounter this

### Scenarios Format

Each scenario includes:
- **Problem Statement**: What's broken?
- **Environment**: Context and setup
- **Symptoms**: What you observe
- **Step-by-step Resolution**: How to debug and fix
- **Root Cause**: Why it happened
- **Prevention**: How to avoid it

### Architecture Exercises Format

Each exercise includes:
- **Requirements**: Functional and non-functional
- **Constraints**: Budget, timeline, compliance
- **Reference Architecture**: Text-based diagram
- **Component Breakdown**: Each component explained
- **Discussion Points**: Trade-offs and alternatives

## Difficulty Levels

- **Junior**: Entry-level positions, 0-2 years experience
- **Mid**: Mid-level positions, 2-5 years experience
- **Senior**: Senior/lead positions, 5+ years experience

Questions may span multiple levels - use your judgment!
